Peperomia Prostrata Care Guide

Soil for Peperomia Prostrata

Well-draining, lightweight soil.

Peperomia Prostrata thrives in well-draining soil with added perlite or sand for improved aeration and drainage.

Watering

Moderate watering; allow topsoil to dry.

Water Peperomia Prostrata when the topsoil feels slightly dry. Avoid overwatering, as this plant prefers slightly drier conditions.

Lighting

Indirect, bright light.

Place Peperomia Prostrata in a location with bright, indirect light. It can tolerate some shade but thrives in well-lit areas.

Benefits

Trailing, ornamental foliage.

Peperomia Prostrata is prized for its trailing vines and unique, small, round leaves, adding beauty to hanging baskets or shelves.

Low-maintenance and adaptable.

This plant is relatively easy to care for and adapts well to various indoor environments, making it suitable for plant enthusiasts of all levels.

Non-Benefits

Avoid overwatering; root rot can occur. Prone to spider mites; monitor regularly and keep humidity levels moderate.
